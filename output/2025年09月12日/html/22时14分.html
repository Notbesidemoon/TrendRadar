Because the iPhone Air’s slimmer design supports only eSIM, Apple initially limited availability to China Unicom, requiring customers to activate the device in carrier stores. The company has indicated that China Mobile and China Telecom will eventually support the model.<br/>Apple’s other new iPhones — the iPhone 17, 17 Pro, and 17 Pro Max — launched in China as planned and include physical SIM cards for the local market.
